Item #: IAP-230

Containment Procedures: IAP-230 cannot be physically contained due to its non-physical, low-observable manifestation state. Instead, containment efforts are focused on monitoring and data suppression.

Internal sleep studies and major neurological research centers are to be monitored via automated keyword filters (e.g., "featureless silhouette," "rebooted dream," "sudden awakening waking amnesia"). Individuals confirmed to have interacted with IAP-230 are to be administered mild sedatives and debriefed. Because IAP-230’s primary effect is self-erasing, Class-A amnestics are rarely required but should be kept on standby if a subject retains anomalous psychological trauma.

Description: IAP-230 is an anomalous humanoid entity that manifests exclusively within human REM sleep cycles.

The entity appears as a tall, stark gray, entirely featureless silhouette. It possesses no distinct facial features, clothing, or defining textures, appearing almost as a rendering error or a "blank space" within the dreamer's environment. Unlike typical dream fabrications, IAP-230 exhibits a rigid, slow, and deliberate walking gait, completely independent of the dream's narrative or logic. It does not speak, vocalize, or acknowledge the dreamer unless directly approached.

IAP-230 does not target specific demographics; its appearances are seemingly random, though it tends to manifest more frequently in highly vivid, surreal, or non-linear dreams. The anomaly's primary effect occurs upon direct visual or physical contact within the dream state. If the dreamer approaches IAP-230, or if IAP-230 walks directly into the dreamer's line of sight at close range, the dream state instantly collapses. Subjects describe a sensation of the dream environment "corrupting" or abruptly cutting to black, followed by immediate awakening.

Upon waking, the subject experiences total, irreversible amnesia regarding the dream they were just having, as well as all others. While a typical person might slowly forget a dream throughout the morning, IAP-230 causes an instantaneous wipe. The subject is consciously aware that they were dreaming vividly a second prior, but cannot recall a single detail, setting, or plot point. Subjects continue being able to recall dreams that happen afterwards, so long as IAP-230 does not appear again.

The only detail that consistently evades this amnestic effect is the memory of the entity itself. Subjects wake up with a lingering, distinct feeling of unease and the vivid recollection of a silent, gray figure standing in a blank void.
